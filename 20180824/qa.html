How can I use Limit and Limit Entry orders to help with my trading?
A limit order is an order to buy or sell an instrument at a specific price or better. A buy limit order can only be executed at the limit price or lower, and a sell limit order can only be executed at the limit price or higher. Is very simple to place a limit order on MT4, you can simply right click on the chart and create a limit order.

General questions for MT4 trading

What happens when I get a margin call on MT4?
The biggest losing position is closed, leaving some free margin available for the trader to continue managing the exposure. Margin calls on MT4 is triggered when the margin level reaches 100%. Margins calls often occur due to overleveraging. Using more leverage can magnify your gains, but it can also magnify losses which will quickly deplete your usable margin. The more leverage you use, the faster your losses can accumulate. When you use excessive leverage, a few losing trades can quickly offset many winning trades.

General questions for MT4 trading

What is margin?
Margin is a good faith deposit required to maintain open positions. In simple terms, it is a portion of your account equity set aside and allocated as a margin deposit. Is not consider a fee or a transaction cost. Margin requirements (per 1k lot) are determined by taking a percentage of the notional trade size plus a small cushion. A small cushion is added to help alleviate daily/weekly fluctuations.
What is a Margin Call?
A Margin Call is a possible event in which a trading account is having losing positions that put him/her on the verge of entering a negative balance in his/her trading account. In order to prevent such a turn of events, a margin call occurs. This is where some of the trader’s open positions are being automatically closed, MT4 automatically closed the biggest losing position thus preventing him from entering into debt.
What is Leverage?
Is a financial tool that lets a trader to increase their market exposure to a level that exceeds the actual investment. Traders use leverage as a way to magnify returns, however is important to notice that losses can potentially magnify as well. Most retail CFDs/FX Brokers offer 200:1 leverage on their trading. GMG believes that the conservative use of leverage is used by majority of successful traders in the market.
What is the relationship between margin and leverage?
Margin is a good faith deposit on open positions, leverage is the amount of market exposure of your positions in relation to your trading investment.
What is the leverage on my account?
It is typically 100:1, if you want to change the leverage on your account you can contact us directly.
How do I know how much margin is required for a trade?
It is about 1% of the notional position size. For example if you place on MT4 a 0.10 EUR/USD position size and the pair is trading at 1.2000. The notional volume of this position is calculated as follows: 10,000 X 1,2000 = $12,000USD. Then $12,000 X 1% = $120 USD is the margin required for this position. It varies depending on each currency pairs, MT4 does the calculation for you, you can check how much margin is required on the margin field on your open positions within the terminal window on MT4.
